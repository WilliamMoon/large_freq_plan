# Orchestrating Computing, Communication, and Agentic AI for Human-Centric Wireless Systems

## Important Dates

**Manuscript Submission Deadline:** 15 August 2026 (Deadline Extended)
**Publication Date:** First Quarter 2027

## Scope

The next generation of wireless systems stands at the confluence of three powerful technological currents: ubiquitous computing, intelligent communication, and agentic artificial intelligence (AI). While prior generations of wireless networks primarily focused on connectivity and spectral efficiency, the emerging vision of 6G and beyond calls for a fundamental paradigm shift — from performance-centric systems to human-centric ones. Future networks must evolve beyond simply connecting devices toward connecting experiences, where communication, computation, and intelligence are cohesively orchestrated to enhance human life and interaction.

In a human-centric wireless ecosystem, the network is designed not merely as infrastructure but as an enabler of human presence, perception, and intention. New applications, such as Haptic-enabled remote health monitoring, Collaborative creative workspaces, digital companionship, and fluid metaverse experiences, heavily demand seamless integration of communication, computing, and intelligence to create hyper-realistic and responsive environments. These scenarios require extremely low-latency, high-fidelity data transmission coupled with distributed intelligence that can adapt to user contexts and cognitive states.

To realize such human-aware networks, communication, computing, and AI must be co-designed and orchestrated. The boundary separating the physical and cognitive worlds will gradually blur as AI-driven agents collaborate with humans in real time, sensing intent, negotiating resources, and dynamically adapting the network to user experience. Emerging agentic AI systems capable of autonomous reasoning, long-term planning, and cooperative optimization, endow thenetworks with higher freedoms to lean on human objectives rather than fitting predefined technical constraints.

This special issue aims to bring together high-quality research contributions that explore new theories, architectures, and enabling technologies for orchestrating **Computing, Communication, and Agentic AI** in **human-centric wireless systems**. We invite original work that redefines how next-generation wireless networks perceive, interact with, and serve human users, aspiring to enhance human experiences across virtual, physical, and cognitive domains.

The topics of interest include but are not limited to:

- Joint source–channel coding for immersive multimedia (e.g., holographic video, XR, VR/AR streaming)
- Cross-layer optimization for ultra-low-latency telepresence and real-time collaboration
- Semantic and intent-aware communications for human-to-human and human-to-machine interaction
- QoE quantification and optimization for metaverse and extended-reality applications
- Edge–cloud continuum architecture for joint source–channel coding and immersive content delivery
- Privacy-Preserving Architectures and Protocols for Cloud-Edge-Device Metaverse Systems
- Green and sustainable computing–communication co-design for human-centric services
- Cross-domain synchronization between networking, sensing, and computing resources for human presence reconstruction
- Agentic AI frameworks for proactive and intent-driven network management
- Multi-agent collaboration and coordination for distributed edge intelligence
- Multi-Modal Sensor Fusion and Knowledge Distillation for Robust Wireless Perception
- Real-Time Distributed Digital Twin Systems for Immersive Networks
- AI-Driven Network Slicing for Personalized Human-Centric Services
- Cognitive and ethical design of AI agents for trustworthy human–AI interaction
- Information-theoretic and signal-processing foundations for joint communication, computing, and learning
- Performance limits and trade-offs in human-intent-aware communication systems
- Emerging standards, testbeds, and prototypes for human-centric wireless systems
